Dr. Norman Borlaug: The Man Who Saved a Billion Lives

A Life of Innovation and Dedication

Dr. Norman Ernest Borlaug (1914 â€“ 2009) was an American agronomist and humanitarian who has been called "the father of the Green Revolution." His innovative work in developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties helped stave off famine in India, Pakistan, and other developing nations.

Key Milestones:

  • 1944: Began his career in Mexico, working on wheat rust, a devastating fungal disease.
  • 1960s: Developed semi-dwarf, high-yield varieties of wheat that were resistant to rust.
  • 1970: Was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to world peace through increased food supply.
  • His Legacy: It is estimated that his work saved over a billion people from starvation.
